Sights

If you come to Lancaster, you can see many places it has to offer like....

Kitchen Kettle Village

Kitchen Kettle Village in Intercourse, Pennsylvania is a small Lancaster County shopping village with over 40 locally owned shops, food stands, and handmade goods. Known for its Jam and Relish Kitchen, Amish-country setting, and seasonal events, it offers a mix of artisan crafts, local foods, and family-friendly activities in a charming, traditional atmosphere.

Dutch Wonderland

Dutch Wonderland, located in Lancaster, Pennsylvania, is a family-friendly amusement park designed especially for young children. Opened in 1963, it features over 35 kid-focused rides, including gentle coasters, water play areas, and interactive attractions.

Sight and Sound Theater

Sight and Sound Theatres in Lancaster, PA is a large Christian theater known for its immersive, Broadway-style productions based on Bible stories. Featuring massive sets, live animals, special effects, and original music, it attracts thousands of visitors for shows like Noah, Moses, Queen Esther, David, and recently added Daniel.

Fulton Theater

The Fulton Theatre, also known as the Fulton Opera House, is a historic landmark in downtown Lancaster and one of the oldest working theaters in the United States. Built in 1852, it is a National Historic Landmark known for its beautifully preserved Victorian architecture and rich theatrical history. Today, the Fulton operates as a professional regional theater producing Broadway-quality musicals, plays, and family shows throughout the year.

Strasburg Rail Road

The Strasburg Rail Road in Strasburg, PA is the oldest operating railroad in the U.S., offering steam-train rides through scenic Amish farmland. Known for its historic locomotives and family-friendly events, it provides a nostalgic, 45-minute countryside experience.

Long Wood Gardens

Longwood Gardens in Kennett Square, PA is a large, world-renowned botanical garden featuring over 1,000 acres of landscaped gardens, meadows, woodlands, and an expansive conservatory. Known for its fountain shows, seasonal displays, and holiday lights, it offers a year-round, immersive nature experience.

Landis Valley Museum

Landis Valley Village and Farm Museum in Lancaster, PA is a living history museum that showcases Pennsylvania German rural life from the 1700s to the early 1900s. The village features historic buildings, farms, artisans, and demonstrations that bring early American culture to life, making it an educational and immersive heritage experience.

The Demuth Museum

The Demuth Museum in Lancaster, PA is dedicated to the life and work of modernist painter Charles Demuth, located in his preserved childhood home and studio. The museum showcases his art, rotating exhibitions, and historical artifacts, offering insight into Demuth’s influence on American modernism and Lancaster’s artistic heritage.

Lancaster Brewing Company

Lancaster Brewing Company is a well-known craft brewery in Lancaster, PA, recognized for its award-winning beers like Milk Stout and Hop Hog IPA. With a focus on local ingredients and small-batch brewing, it offers a taproom and restaurant where visitors can enjoy fresh brews alongside pub-style food in a relaxed, historic setting.

Historic St. Mary's Catholic Church

Historic St. Mary’s Catholic Church in Lancaster, PA is the oldest Catholic parish in the city, dating back to the mid-1700s. Known for its beautiful Gothic-style architecture, stained-glass windows, and centuries-old heritage, the church remains an active place of worship and an important landmark in Lancaster’s religious and cultural history.
